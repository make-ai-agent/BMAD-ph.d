# Research Quality Checklist

## Purpose

This checklist ensures research meets high academic standards for novelty, rigor, and impact suitable for top-tier data science conferences.

## Research Foundation

- [ ] **Novel Research Question**: The research addresses a genuinely new problem or provides a novel approach to existing problems
- [ ] **Clear Motivation**: The importance and relevance of the research question is well-established
- [ ] **Comprehensive Literature Review**: All major related work is cited and properly positioned
- [ ] **Identified Research Gap**: The specific gap in existing knowledge is clearly articulated
- [ ] **Theoretical Grounding**: The work builds on solid theoretical foundations

## Methodology Rigor

- [ ] **Sound Experimental Design**: Experiments are well-designed with appropriate controls
- [ ] **Clear Hypotheses**: Research hypotheses are clearly stated and testable
- [ ] **Appropriate Baselines**: Relevant and strong baseline methods are included
- [ ] **Fair Comparisons**: All methods are evaluated under identical conditions
- [ ] **Statistical Validity**: Results include statistical significance testing where appropriate
- [ ] **Ablation Studies**: Key components are systematically analyzed

## Technical Quality

- [ ] **Algorithmic Clarity**: Algorithms are clearly described with pseudocode
- [ ] **Theoretical Analysis**: Complexity analysis or theoretical guarantees provided where applicable
- [ ] **Implementation Details**: Sufficient details for reproduction are included
- [ ] **Parameter Settings**: All hyperparameters and settings are documented
- [ ] **Code Availability**: Plans for code release are stated

## Results and Evaluation

- [ ] **Comprehensive Evaluation**: Multiple datasets/scenarios are tested
- [ ] **Appropriate Metrics**: Evaluation metrics are suitable for the problem
- [ ] **Error Analysis**: Failure cases and limitations are analyzed
- [ ] **Result Significance**: Improvements are meaningful, not just marginal
- [ ] **Honest Reporting**: Both positive and negative results are reported

## Reproducibility

- [ ] **Dataset Availability**: Datasets are publicly available or clearly described
- [ ] **Preprocessing Steps**: Data preprocessing is fully documented
- [ ] **Random Seeds**: Random seeds are fixed and reported
- [ ] **Computational Requirements**: Hardware and time requirements are stated
- [ ] **Version Information**: Software versions and dependencies are listed

## Ethical Considerations

- [ ] **Ethics Statement**: Potential ethical implications are discussed
- [ ] **Data Privacy**: Privacy concerns are addressed if applicable
- [ ] **Bias Analysis**: Potential biases in data or methods are examined
- [ ] **Broader Impact**: Positive and negative societal impacts are considered
- [ ] **Responsible Disclosure**: Sensitive findings are handled appropriately

## Scientific Integrity

- [ ] **Honest Limitations**: Limitations are clearly acknowledged
- [ ] **No Cherry-Picking**: Results are not selectively reported
- [ ] **Appropriate Claims**: Claims match the evidence provided
- [ ] **Proper Attribution**: All contributions are properly attributed
- [ ] **Conflict of Interest**: Any conflicts of interest are declared

## Innovation and Impact

- [ ] **Technical Innovation**: The work introduces new techniques or insights
- [ ] **Practical Applicability**: Real-world applications are demonstrated or discussed
- [ ] **Generalizability**: The approach generalizes beyond specific examples
- [ ] **Future Directions**: Promising future research directions are identified
- [ ] **Community Value**: The work will be valuable to the research community

## Review Readiness

- [ ] **Addresses Likely Concerns**: Anticipates and addresses reviewer concerns
- [ ] **Competitive Results**: Results are competitive with state-of-the-art
- [ ] **Clear Contributions**: Contributions are explicitly stated and supported
- [ ] **Appropriate Scope**: The scope is suitable for the target venue
- [ ] **Technical Depth**: Sufficient technical depth for the conference standards

## Additional Conference-Specific Requirements

- [ ] **Format Compliance**: Follows conference formatting guidelines
- [ ] **Anonymization**: Properly anonymized for double-blind review
- [ ] **Supplementary Material**: Appropriate use of supplementary material
- [ ] **Length Constraints**: Meets page/word limits
- [ ] **Special Sections**: Includes required sections (reproducibility, ethics, etc.)
