# Research Ideation Task

## ⚠️ CRITICAL EXECUTION NOTICE ⚠️

**THIS IS AN EXECUTABLE WORKFLOW - NOT REFERENCE MATERIAL**

When this task is invoked:

1. **MANDATORY STEP-BY-STEP EXECUTION** - Each phase must be processed sequentially with user feedback
2. **ELICITATION IS REQUIRED** - User interaction is mandatory at key decision points
3. **NO SHORTCUTS ALLOWED** - Complete ideation requires following this workflow

## Task Overview

This task guides the research ideation process for academic papers, helping identify novel research questions, assess feasibility, and establish theoretical foundations.

## Activation

When user requests research ideation or runs `*ideate` command, begin this workflow immediately.

## Inputs

- **Topic Area** (required): General field or problem space
- **Conference Target** (optional): Specific conference requirements
- **Constraints** (optional): Resource, time, or scope limitations

## Process

### Phase 1: Topic Exploration

1. **Brainstorm Initial Directions**

   - Generate 5-7 potential research angles
   - Consider interdisciplinary connections
   - Identify trending topics in the field

2. **Present Options with Rationale**
   - Explain potential impact of each direction
   - Note feasibility considerations
   - Highlight novelty aspects

**ELICITATION REQUIRED**: Present numbered list of research directions and wait for user selection or feedback.

### Phase 2: Literature Gap Analysis

1. **Identify Related Work**

   - List key papers in selected direction
   - Note methodologies used
   - Identify limitations of existing work

2. **Find Research Gaps**

   - What questions remain unanswered?
   - What methods haven't been tried?
   - What datasets are underexplored?

3. **Present Gap Analysis**
   - Prioritize gaps by impact and feasibility
   - Suggest specific research questions

**ELICITATION REQUIRED**: Present gap analysis and proposed research questions. Wait for user refinement.

### Phase 3: Hypothesis Generation

1. **Formulate Testable Hypotheses**

   - Create 2-3 specific hypotheses
   - Ensure measurability
   - Define success criteria

2. **Design Preliminary Experiments**
   - Outline experimental approach
   - Identify required resources
   - Estimate timeline

**ELICITATION REQUIRED**: Present hypotheses and experimental design. Get user approval or modifications.

### Phase 4: Feasibility Assessment

1. **Resource Requirements**

   - Computational needs
   - Data availability
   - Time estimates
   - Expertise required

2. **Risk Analysis**

   - Technical risks
   - Timeline risks
   - Novelty vs. acceptance trade-offs

3. **Make Recommendation**
   - Go/no-go decision with rationale
   - Alternative approaches if needed

## Outputs

- **Research Proposal Document** containing:
  - Selected research question
  - Literature gap justification
  - Hypotheses and experiments
  - Feasibility assessment
  - Preliminary timeline

## Collaboration Points

- **With Analyst Agent**: For data availability and analysis feasibility
- **With Paper Expert**: For conference fit and scope alignment
- **With Existing Projects**: Check for synergies or conflicts

## Quality Criteria

- Novelty: Is this genuinely new?
- Impact: Will this advance the field?
- Feasibility: Can this be completed?
- Clarity: Is the question well-defined?

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

- Too broad scope
- Insufficient novelty
- Unrealistic resource requirements
- Weak theoretical grounding
- Missing related work
